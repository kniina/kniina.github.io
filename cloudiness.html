Cloudiness

Cloudiness varies with distance from the equator. Generally, cities with the most cloud coverage are located between the tropics starting at latitude 23.5° (the Tropic of Cancer and Tropic of Capricorn) and the temperate zones (normally referring to latitudes 35–66.5°) north and south of the Equator. However, based on the current analysis, cities in the Northern hemisphere experiencing Summer tended to be more cloudy than those in the Southern hemisphere experiencing Winter.
